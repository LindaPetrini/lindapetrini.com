About

Linda Petrini

AI researcher, technical writer, and coach.

I'm a researcher, writer, and coach working at the intersection of AI and human experience. I care about what the fast acceleration of AI development does to people — the confusion, the disorientation, the grief, the adaptation — and I try to hold both sides: the technical and the deeply human. I translate complex ideas into accessible stories, and I help people understand and process what is actually going on.

How I got here

I spent four years as an AI researcher, working on interpretability — trying to understand what neural networks are actually doing — and applying those methods to decoding epigenomics. It was absorbing, intense work, and it eventually led to burnout. That became a turning point. I went deep into coaching, embodiment, and somatic exploration, and what came out the other side was more integrated: a life that holds research, writing, teaching, and practice together, rather than flattening everything into one lane.

What I care about now

The thing I keep coming back to is the gap between how AI is talked about and how it is actually experienced. The technical discourse is fast, confident, and often disconnected from what people feel — the anxiety, the wonder, the sense of something shifting underfoot. I want to close that gap. Not by simplifying the technology, but by taking both sides seriously.

Background

I freelance as a technical writer and researcher, with work published through Palisade Research, Foresight Institute, Anthropic, and the Bezos Earth Fund, and peer-reviewed research at ICLR. I also teach acroyoga and am studying Rolfing. I live in a pretty rural part of Italy, part of a community of digital nomads — a semi-nomadic life that suits me well. I write about AI and life more broadly on my Substack.
